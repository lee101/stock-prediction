## Leverage sensitivity — 16-seed vs prior cells

Marginal median per +0.25 leverage (implied slope over the 1.0→1.25 step):
- **16-seed alltrain 400/0.03**: (52.21 − 40.44) / 0.25 = **+47.08 %/mo per unit leverage**
- 5-seed old sweet-spot (general champion, not alltrain): +34.08 %/mo per unit leverage
- v7 RL leverage ceiling (memory: caps at +17.5%/mo @ 3×): **negative at the margin** above 1.5× (p10 collapses, max DD 19.4%)

XGB 16-seed is 1.4× steeper in leverage response than the 5-seed pre-alltrain champion and qualitatively stronger than any RL ensemble we've tested. The concentrated top_n=1 daily signal scales almost linearly — multiplying the bet size multiplies the return without diluting hit rate (n_neg 2/30 unchanged).

## Interpretation

- **Leverage is the highest-leverage PnL knob we have**, more so than seed averaging (+1.59 med) or LR regime (+1.07 med at 16 seeds).
- The DD cost is predictable: 1.25× leverage on a strategy with 31.44% worst DD ≈ 39.3% expected, observed 38.55%. No leverage-induced tail blowup.
- p10 actually improves vs 16-seed lev=1.0 (5.34 > 4.74) because the left tail is not lev-negative — the two negative windows scale proportionally.
- sortino effectively flat (19.56 vs 19.63) because downside stdev also scales with leverage.
